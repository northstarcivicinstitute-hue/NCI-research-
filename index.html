About NCI

We Build What
Others Don't.

Northstar Civic Institute is a nonpartisan civic research and policy organization based in Michigan. We don't advocate. We research, design, and build infrastructure — then produce the evidence that moves policy and practice forward.

Founded by Donavan Norman, NCI operates at the intersection of youth development, sports equity, agricultural access, and civic leadership — four pillars that together address the systemic gaps holding Michigan communities back.

We are state incorporated with an active EIN and a federal 501(c)(3) application in process, with active pilots in the field across Saginaw, Tuscola, and Bay Counties.

Youth Athletics + Behavioral Health

PlayStrong

A six-month pilot program using structured youth athletics as the entry point for social-emotional learning and behavioral health intervention — reaching youth who might never otherwise access those services.

Program Design

Sport as the Entry Point for the Support System

PlayStrong is built on a simple but underutilized insight: young people show up for sport even when they won't show up for mental health services. By embedding SEL curriculum and behavioral health referral pathways directly into athletic programming, we reach youth at the moment of engagement — before crisis, not after.

The pilot operates across Saginaw, Tuscola, and Bay Counties, targeting youth in under-resourced communities where behavioral health access is most limited and sports infrastructure is already present but underutilized.

🏃
Structured Athletic Programming

Organized sport as the consistent, trusted touchpoint for youth engagement across the six-month cycle

🧠
SEL Curriculum Integration

Social-emotional learning woven into practice sessions and team activities — not as a separate program

🔗
Behavioral Health Referral Pathways

Structured warm-handoff protocols connecting youth and families to licensed behavioral health providers

📊
Outcomes Documentation

Rigorous data collection on SEL skill development, referral rates, and service uptake for evidence building

Research & Policy

The Full Spectrum of Sports Equity — Not Just NIL

MSEEM began as a response to Michigan's extension of NIL rights to high school athletes. But the deeper story is broader: for every student navigating NIL, there are dozens locked out of Olympic pathway sports like swimming, gymnastics, fencing, and wrestling — not because of talent, but because of cost.

NCI is building the research infrastructure Michigan needs: district-level equity audits, compliance tools, and policy analysis that makes the invisible visible — and actionable.

📋
NIL Compliance Framework

District-ready tools helping Michigan schools navigate NIL rights equitably following legislative extension

🏊
Olympic Pathway Access Research

Audit of participation barriers in wrestling, swimming, gymnastics, track, and other Olympic sports by district demographics

💰
Pay-to-Play Barrier Analysis

Systematic documentation of fee structures, equipment costs, and travel burdens across Michigan athletic programs

📈
Economic Mobility Outcomes

Connecting sports access data to scholarship, career pathway, and economic mobility outcomes at the district level

Land as the Entry Point for Equity

FFA and 4-H are powerful youth development infrastructures — but they've been associated almost exclusively with rural, majority-white communities. GroundWork changes that. By building intentional urban satellite chapters and activating underutilized land through county land bank partnerships, we create a cross-community pipeline nobody else is building in Michigan.

This is not an agricultural program. It is an equity infrastructure project that uses land as the entry point — connecting urban youth to career pathways in agricultural technology, environmental science, and land entrepreneurship while revitalizing rural participation through new community partnerships.

🌆
FFA & 4-H Urban Expansion

Satellite programs in Saginaw, Bay City, and Flint with culturally adapted curriculum and peer mentorship pathways

🏗️
Land Activation

Partnering with county land banks and MSU Extension to convert defunct parcels into working agricultural training sites

👥
Cross-Community Cohort Model

Structured experiences mixing urban, suburban, and rural youth around real land-based projects and shared outcomes

Two-Way Pipeline

Reshaping the Pipeline from Both Ends

Michigan's public institutions face a deepening civic workforce gap. CivicForge doesn't just try to fill it from one direction — it runs the pipeline both ways at once. Young people are trained to enter public service; existing public servants are trained to receive and retain them. The result is a reinforcing system that builds institutional capacity while building careers.

Youth & Young Adults → Public Service
  • Civic literacy curriculum for HS students (14–18)
  • Paid fellowship placements in partner agencies (18–26)
  • Credentialing support through CMU & Delta College
  • Mentorship with active public servants
⇅
Educators & Civil Servants → Youth-Ready
  • Trauma-informed youth engagement training
  • Cultural competency & motivational interviewing
  • Building internal youth pipelines
  • MiLEAP-aligned CEU / recertification credit

Legislative Context

Michigan Policy
Landscape

NCI actively tracks and informs Michigan legislation across youth development, sports equity, and education policy. We serve as a nonpartisan research resource for legislators and their staff.

HB 4643
High School NIL Rights Implementation
Governs the framework for Name, Image, and Likeness rights for Michigan high school athletes following the state's extension of NIL protections. NCI tracks district-level compliance gaps and equity implications.
Active — NCI Research Aligned
HB 4371
Youth Athletic Access & Pay-to-Play Reform
Addresses access barriers in Michigan youth athletic programs including fee structures and program equity requirements. NCI's MSEEM research directly informs the equity analysis supporting this legislation.
Active — NCI Research Aligned
SB 0014
Youth Sports Economic Mobility Framework
Senate legislation addressing the economic mobility implications of youth sports access disparities across Michigan. NCI serves as a nonpartisan research resource for Senate offices tracking this bill.
Active — NCI Monitoring
