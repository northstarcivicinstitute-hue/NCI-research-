Why this split works

Keep NCI as the sponsor. Let the Lab lead the visible work.

This structure preserves institutional credibility while making Community Equity Lab the operational brand for pilots, community engagement, and practitioner-facing tools.

Legislative brief
“The Michigan Youth Activity Equity Act is strongest as a transparency, parity, and grant-enabling statute rather than as a large new mandate.”
Institutional sponsor needed for state-level policy packaging
MSEEM scorecard brief
“The MSEEM Sports Access and Equity Scorecard is best understood as measurement infrastructure rather than as a stand-alone report card.”
Technical infrastructure benefits from a credible research home
CivicForge brief
“CivicForge helps Michigan communities build a stronger pathway into education and public service by pairing youth pipeline development with institutional readiness.”
Operational pilots read best as a named program
Site architecture

Exact placement that works across homepage, briefs, and partner pages

Use NCI as the global institutional header, then surface Community Equity Lab as the prominent program-level hero and initiative identity.

1
Global header

NCI / Northstar Civic Institute

Keep the institutional author visible at the top of every page. Add a persistent Request Briefing path for funders, legislators, and researchers.

2
Homepage hero

Community Equity Lab

Lead with the program identity, the short tagline, and a small line that makes the NCI relationship explicit.

3
About page

Governance plus credibility

Place the 40-word description beneath the institutional mission and keep the one-line author credit prominent.

4
Initiatives and briefs

Separate operations from technical infrastructure

Place toolkits and pilot materials under the Lab. Place methods, data appendices, and scorecards under NCI.

5
Contact routing

Operational ownership stays clear

Route pilot and partner requests through Community Equity Lab while preserving NCI as the institutional umbrella.

6
Visual split

Institutional and humanized

Use NCI in the global frame and Community Equity Lab in the warm, action-oriented sections and calls to action.

Ready-to-paste copy

Public-facing language for the site, briefs, and contact points

These blocks are already placed across the site and also included in the package so you can reuse them in your CMS, decks, or one-pagers.

Hero tagline
Community Equity Lab — translating rigorous research into equitable school solutions.
Author credit
Community Equity Lab, a program of NCI — leading equity-centered pilot operations, community engagement, and independent evaluation.
40-word description
Community Equity Lab partners with schools, families, and districts to translate rigorous research into practice: we design and run pilots, center lived experience in evaluation, and produce actionable policy guidance that reduces disparities in student mental health and preserves youth sports access.
Contact microcopy
For pilot partnerships, training, or data requests, contact Community Equity Lab at Donavan@nciresearch.org. For research and policy dissemination contact NCI via the same address until separate inboxes are established.
Recommended implementation order

Homepage first, briefs second, initiative downloads next.

Add the hero block, add the byline to one-pagers and slide decks, then publish the initiative landing page with separate Lab and NCI download paths.
