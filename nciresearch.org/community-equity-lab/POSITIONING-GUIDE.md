# Community Equity Lab positioning guide

## Recommendation
Keep NCI as the institutional header and surface Community Equity Lab prominently as the program-level hero and initiative header. This preserves institutional credibility while making the Lab the visible operational brand for pilots, community engagement, and practitioner tools.

## Why this split works
- Legislative brief: “The Michigan Youth Activity Equity Act is strongest as a transparency, parity, and grant-enabling statute rather than as a large new mandate.”
- MSEEM scorecard brief: “The MSEEM Sports Access and Equity Scorecard is best understood as measurement infrastructure rather than as a stand-alone report card.”
- CivicForge brief: “CivicForge helps Michigan communities build a stronger pathway into education and public service by pairing youth pipeline development with institutional readiness.”

## Exact copy
### Hero tagline
Community Equity Lab — translating rigorous research into equitable school solutions.

### Author credit
Community Equity Lab, a program of NCI — leading equity-centered pilot operations, community engagement, and independent evaluation.

### 40-word program description
Community Equity Lab partners with schools, families, and districts to translate rigorous research into practice: we design and run pilots, center lived experience in evaluation, and produce actionable policy guidance that reduces disparities in student mental health and preserves youth sports access.

## Where it lives in the package
- `index.html` uses the short hero tagline.
- `about.html` uses the 40-word description and role split.
- `briefs.html` uses the brief header and evidence excerpts.
- `initiatives.html` separates Lab toolkits from NCI technical appendices.
- `contact.html` routes through Donavan@nciresearch.org while keeping Community Equity Lab as the operational label.

## Brand notes
- Sitewide institutional frame: NCI / Northstar Civic Institute
- Brand colors: Navy #1B2A4A, Gold #C5A55A (matches NCI parent site)
- Program-level visual brand: Community Equity Lab
- Logo assets: `assets/logo-mark.svg`, `assets/logo-lockup.svg`
- Public tone: formal on research pages, humanized on initiative pages
